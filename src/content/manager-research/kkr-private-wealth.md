## Investment Solutions

### K-PEC Private Equity - Class I & D

**Asset Class**: Private Equity

K-PEC provides access to KKR's private equity platform through an interval fund structure, combining primary funds, co-investments, and secondaries.

**Key Features:**
- **Structure**: Interval Fund with quarterly liquidity
- **Investment Strategy**: Diversified private equity including primary funds, co-investments, and secondaries
- **Share Classes**: 
  - **Class D**: Available to all investors
  - **Class I**: Accredited investors only
- **Focus**: Long-term capital appreciation through private company ownership
- **Liquidity**: Quarterly redemption windows

**Note**: Class I shares require accredited investor status (generally $1M net worth excluding primary residence or $200K/$300K income).

### KREST REIT - Class D & I

**Asset Class**: Real Estate

KKR Real Estate Select Trust (KREST) provides access to KKR's institutional real estate platform through a non-traded REIT structure.

**Key Features:**
- **Structure**: Non-Traded REIT
- **Investment Strategy**: Diversified real estate across property types and geographies
- **Share Classes**:
  - **Class D**: Available to all investors
  - **Class I**: Accredited investors only
- **Focus**: Income generation and capital appreciation
- **Approach**: Institutional real estate across risk/return spectrum

### K-INFRA - Class I & D

**Asset Class**: Infrastructure

KKR Infrastructure Fund provides access to essential infrastructure assets with predictable cash flows.

**Key Features:**
- **Structure**: Interval Fund
- **Investment Strategy**: Core and core-plus infrastructure with steady cash flows
- **Share Classes**:
  - **Class D**: Available to all investors
  - **Class I**: Accredited investors only
- **Focus**: Income generation with inflation protection
- **Sectors**: Energy, transportation, utilities, and digital infrastructure

**Note**: Class I shares require accredited investor status.

## Understanding Share Classes

KKR offers multiple share classes to accommodate different investor types:

**Class D Shares:**
- Available to all investors (non-accredited and accredited)
- Higher expense ratios to account for distribution costs
- No accreditation requirements

**Class I Shares:**
- Available only to accredited investors
- Lower expense ratios (typically ~0.50-1.00% lower than Class D)
- Requires meeting accreditation thresholds

*Accredited Investor Definition*: Generally requires either $1M net worth (excluding primary residence) OR $200K individual/$300K joint annual income for the past 2 years with expectation of continuing.
